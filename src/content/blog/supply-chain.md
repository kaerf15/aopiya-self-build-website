Transparency in the leather industry is rare. We believe you have the right to know exactly where your bag comes from.

## The Tannery: Tuscany, Italy

Our leather journey begins in the Conceria region of Tuscany, where our partner tannery has operated since 1958. They specialize in vegetable tanning — a 40-day process using natural tannins from oak, chestnut, and mimosa bark.

## Certification

- ** Vegetable Tanning Association** certified
- **Leather Working Group** gold-rated
- Traceable hide sourcing from European farms

## The Journey

1. **Raw hides** arrive from certified European farms
2. **Tanning** — 40 days in wooden drums with natural tannins
3. **Drying** — air-dried on wooden frames, never forced
4. **Finishing** — dyed and treated with natural waxes
5. **Shipping** — sea freight to Guangzhou (lower carbon footprint than air)
6. **Inspection** — every hide hand-checked by our lead artisan

## Our Atelier: Guangzhou

In a quiet district of Guangzhou, five artisans work in a former textile workshop. Each bag passes through a single pair of hands from start to finish — no assembly lines, no outsourcing.

The leather arrives. The artisan selects the hide. The cutting begins. Forty hours later, a bag exists where none did before.
