Patina is the soul of leather. It is the visible record of every raindrop, every hour of sunlight, every hand that has held the bag. Unlike synthetic materials that simply wear out, vegetable-tanned leather grows more beautiful with age.

## What Is Patina?

Patina is the surface changes that occur on leather through oxidation, exposure to light, and contact with oils from human skin. Over months and years, the leather darkens, softens, and develops a subtle sheen that cannot be replicated by any factory process.

## How to Cultivate a Beautiful Patina

**Use it daily.** The best patina comes from regular use. Carry your bag to work, on weekend trips, to the grocery store. Every journey leaves its mark.

**Embrace the elements.** Light rain will not harm vegetable-tanned leather — it may even accelerate the patina process. Simply let it air dry naturally, away from direct heat.

**Handle with clean hands.** The natural oils from your skin are the primary driver of patina. Clean hands ensure an even, beautiful development rather than uneven dark spots.

## What to Avoid

- Prolonged exposure to direct sunlight (causes uneven fading)
- Contact with oils, perfumes, or alcohol-based products
- Storing in plastic bags (traps moisture)
- Using harsh leather conditioners

A well-patinated bag is a diary written in leather. No two bags age the same way — yours will be entirely unique.
